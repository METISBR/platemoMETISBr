# PlatEMO Fork by METISBr: Comprehensive Overview

## Definition and Purpose

A **fork** in software development represents an independent derivative of a source code repository, wherein developers create a distinct divergence from the original project to pursue specialized objectives while maintaining the foundational architecture and methodological framework. In the context of the **platemoMETISBr** initiative, this fork constitutes a specialized adaptation of the original [PlatEMO platform](https://github.com/BIMK/PlatEMO) — an extensively comprehensive MATLAB-based optimization framework — that has been customized to accommodate the research imperatives and algorithmic innovations of **METISBr** (Grupo de Pesquisa em Otimização Multi-Objetivo e Many-Objective).

## METISBr: Institutional Context

**METISBr** is a Brazilian research collective dedicated to advancing the theoretical and practical dimensions of **Multi-Objective Optimization (MOO)** and **Many-Objective Optimization (MaOPs)**. The group's scholarly endeavors encompass:

- **Algorithm development and refinement** for evolutionary multi-objective optimization
- **Comparative performance analysis** across heterogeneous optimization methodologies
- **Domain-specific applications** of MOO/MaOPs within Brazilian and Latin American contexts
- **Scalability investigations** pertaining to high-dimensional objective spaces

## Characteristics of the platemoMETISBr Fork

This derivative maintains structural fidelity to the original PlatEMO architecture while incorporating:

1. **Proprietary Algorithms**: Potentially augmented or novel algorithmic implementations developed by the research collective
2. **Customized Experimental Frameworks**: Experimental configurations optimized for specific research hypotheses
3. **Extended Documentation**: Localized documentation and methodological descriptions in Portuguese
4. **Community Contributions**: Collaborative enhancements reflecting the group's research trajectory

## Technical Infrastructure

The fork preserves the essential modular composition of PlatEMO:

- **Algorithm Repository**: Comprehensive implementation of evolutionary algorithms (NSGA-II, NSGA-III, MOEA/D, etc.)
- **Problem Definitions**: Multi-objective benchmark problems and user-defined problem interfaces
- **Performance Metrics**: Quantitative evaluation criteria (Hypervolume, IGD, Spacing, etc.)
- **GUI Components**: Interactive visualization and experimental configuration interfaces

## Significance

This fork exemplifies the **reproducibility and extensibility paradigm** inherent in open-source scientific software, enabling the METISBr collective to maintain research independence while leveraging the robustness and maturity of the original PlatEMO framework.
